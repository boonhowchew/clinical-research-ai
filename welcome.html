The problem this solves

You're using AI. But is it helping — or quietly misleading you?

Most clinician-researchers have started experimenting with ChatGPT or Claude. Some have found it useful. Others have submitted manuscripts with fabricated references they didn't notice, produced literature reviews with no confirmed gap, or spent months on a proposal AI cheerfully encouraged but that had no chance of publication.

AI is a genuinely powerful research partner — but it carries two distinct families of risk: process risk (it invents the path as it goes, with no research system) and output risk (it fabricates citations, miscalculates statistics, and presents outdated guidelines as current fact). This module teaches you to harness what it does well and protect yourself from what it gets wrong.
