Part 1A — Quantitative Proposal Preparation
Research Proposal Preparation (Quantitative)
Comprehensive 18-step guide from research idea to submission-ready quantitative proposal. Covers the 16-section proposal outline (Sections A–Q), grant call intelligence, AI-assisted literature review with multiple tools (7-step process), conceptual framework, PICO/PECOTS research question formulation, bias & validity, sampling, sample size, data analysis plan, ethics, and research vs audit distinctions. Includes LLM risk-of-bias assessment evidence and 69+ expandable concept cards.
18 proposal steps Grant call AI strategy Literature review PICO/PECOTS Bias & validity Sample size Ethics

Qualitative Research Proposal Preparation
Parallel qualitative proposal guide covering philosophical foundations (ontology, epistemology, methodology), five qualitative designs (phenomenology, grounded theory, IPA, thematic analysis, case study), SPIDER framework for research questions, purposive sampling strategies, saturation, data collection methods, qualitative data analysis, and trustworthiness criteria including COREQ and member checking.

Part 2A — Quantitative Data to Publication
Data to Publication (Quantitative)
From completed dataset to submitted manuscript. Covers AI scientific anchor setup, variables & measurement, missing data strategies, statistical analysis & interpretation (descriptive, inferential, regression, Cox, survival), data visualisation, IMRAD manuscript drafting (Results, Discussion, Conclusion, Introduction, Methods), cover letter, AI disclosure, the integrity gate, and replicability standards. Aligned to CONSORT, STROBE, and PRISMA reporting.

Part 2B — Qualitative Data to Publication
Qualitative Data to Publication
Parallel qualitative journey from transcripts to published paper. Covers qualitative AI anchor, transcript management & anonymisation, saturation assessment, thematic analysis (coding → theme development → negative case analysis), data display, qualitative manuscript conventions (Findings, Discussion, Conclusion), COREQ reporting, transferability, and thick description. Includes integrity gate for pre-submission audit.
Transcript management Thematic analysis Negative case analysis COREQ reporting Transferability Integrity gate

Learning Outcomes

MMC CPD Domains
Outcome 1 · Medical Knowledge
Explain the role of large language models in clinical research and identify their two distinct failure families (process risk and output risk), applying appropriate safeguards at each stage of the research workflow.
Medical Knowledge
Outcome 2 · Research Skills
Construct a submission-ready quantitative or qualitative research proposal using the 16-section outline (Sections A–Q), integrating AI tools appropriately for literature review, gap analysis, and methodology design.
Research Skills
Outcome 3 · Research Skills
Apply the correct statistical analysis, data visualisation approach, and manuscript section conventions to produce a structured IMRAD manuscript from a completed quantitative or qualitative dataset.
Research Skills
Outcome 4 · Professionalism
Demonstrate responsible AI use in research and publication — including AI disclosure, linguistic de-biasing, integrity gate checks, and adherence to ICMJE authorship criteria and COPE publication ethics standards.
Professionalism
Outcome 5 · Communication
Produce publication-ready scientific prose applying clarity, accuracy, and conciseness principles; select an appropriate target journal using EQUATOR reporting guidelines; and navigate the full peer-review and resubmission cycle.
Communication
Outcome 6 · Research Skills
Critically appraise AI-generated research outputs at each stage — evaluating signalling-question accuracy in risk-of-bias assessment, verifying statistical calculations, and auditing reference lists for hallucinated citations.
Research Skills

Assessment & Verification

Assignment-based
Completion is verified by the module providers. Each module page contains interactive expandable concept cards, AI prompt exercises, and reference materials. Learners are expected to engage with all 8 pages and apply the learning to their own current or planned research through submitted assignments. A completion certificate will be issued by the module providers upon review of submitted assignments and confirmation of satisfactory completion. The certificate, signed by the module facilitator, may be used for CPD portfolio documentation and MMC CPD point claims.
